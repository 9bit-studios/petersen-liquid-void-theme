# The Petersen Games Story

## Where Legendary Design Meets Cosmic Horror

### It Started with a Phone Call...

In 1980, a young Sandy Petersen received a call that would change gaming forever. Chaosium needed someone to create a horror RPG. Sandy, a devoted Lovecraft fan and experienced game master, said yes.

The result? **Call of Cthulhu** - now in its 7th edition and considered one of the greatest RPGs ever created.

But Sandy was just getting started.

### From Tabletop to Digital Legend

You might know Sandy's digital work:

| Game | Role | Company |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **DOOM** | Designed 19 levels | id Software |
| **DOOM II** | Designed 17 levels | id Software |
| **QUAKE** | Lead Designer | id Software |
| **Age of Empires** | Designer | Ensemble Studios |

*"I've been scaring players for 40 years - just switching between cardboard and pixels."*

### The Petersen Games Era

In 2012, Sandy decided to return to his tabletop roots with a crazy idea: What if the players WERE the cosmic horrors?

**Cthulhu Wars** raised $1.4 million on Kickstarter, launching Petersen Games into the stratosphere.

Since then, we've created:

- 12 successful Kickstarter campaigns
- Over $10 million raised from 40,000+ backers
- 5 core game lines with 50+ expansions
- The most detailed miniatures in tabletop gaming

### Our Philosophy

### "Make games I want to play"

It's that simple. Every Petersen Games product must pass the Sandy test: Would I play this every week?

### Asymmetric or Bust

Why make factions that play the same? Every game features radically different strategies.

### Premium Everything

From 180mm Great Old Ones to linen-finished cards, we don't do "good enough."

### Community First

Our 10,000+ cultists aren't customers - they're collaborators. Their feedback shapes every game.

### The Team

While Sandy provides creative vision, Petersen Games is a family affair:

- **Lincoln Petersen** - CEO (Sandy's son)
- **Arthur Petersen** - Lead Developer (Sandy's son)
- **And 20+ talented individuals** bringing cosmic horror to your table
